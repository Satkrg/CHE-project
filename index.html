What is Air Pollution?

Air pollution refers to any physical, chemical or biological change in the air.
It is the contamination of air by harmful gases, dust and smoke which
affects plants, animals and humans drastically.

Types of Air Pollution

  • Indoor Air Pollution

    Indoor Air Pollution means contamination of air quality within and around buildings and structures.
    Some of the most common examples of indoor
    air pollutants are solvents, moulds, smoke, pesticides, gases, and pet dander.

  • Outdoor Air Pollution

    Outdoor Pollution refers to the presence of harmful pollutants in the air outside that can be harmful to human health and the environment. These pollutants can from a variety of sources, such as vehicle exhaust, industrial emissions, construction Sites, and natural sources such as dust and wildfires.

What causes Air Pollution

Air Pollution is caused by variety of human and natural factors.Some of the major causes of air pollution include:


  • Industrial emissions:Industrial that emits pollutants such as sulfur Dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter contribute significantly to air pollution.

  • Transportation Emissions:Vehicles that burn fossil fuels, such as gasoline and diesel, emit polluntants such as Carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter.

  • Agriculture activities:Agriculture practices such as burning crop residues, using fertilizers, and raising livestock can release pollutants into the air.

  • Burning of Fossil fuels:The burning of coal, oil, and natural gas for electicity and heating purposes releases polluntants such as carbon dioxide, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen oxides.

  • Forest Fires:Forest fires release large amounts of smoke and particulate matter into the air.

  • Natural Sources:Dust, volcanic eruptions, and other natural events can also contribute to air pollution.

  • Indoor Sources:Indoor sources such as tobacco smoke, cooking fumes, and household cleaning products can also contribute to air pollution.

How to Contol Air Pollution?

Air pollution is a serious environmental issue that can have negative effects on human health, climate change, and ecosystems. There are several ways that individuals, communities , and governments can help to control air pollution.Some of the most effective ways to reduce air pollution include:

  • Increase Public Awareness: Educating the public about the dangers of air pollution and encouraging them to take action can help raise awareness and lead to more widespread efforts to control air pollution. We can use AirAware.com to increase public awareness.
  • Reduce Vehicular emissions:Transportation is a major contribution to air pollution. Reducing the use of personal vehicles, promoting the use of public transportation, and using hybrid cars can significantly reduce vehicular emissions.
  • Encourage green practices:Promote environmentally-friendly practices like recycling, composting, and reducing the use of single-use plastics to help reduce air polltuion.
  • Plant trees and greenery:Trees absorb carbon dioxide and produce oxygen , which helps to reduce the amount of pollutants in the air.Planting trees and other vegetation can help improve air quality.
